About

I am a Computer Science student specializing in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Malaya. I believe the most interesting problems sit at the boundary of technology and large-scale systems - how digital infrastructure gets built, how capital flows into it, and how institutions make decisions under uncertainty.

I have been fortunate to see this from the inside: I spent six months at Khazanah Nasional, Malaysia's sovereign wealth fund, working in the Managing Director's Office on technology strategy tied to national investment and policy. That experience shaped how I think about the gap between what technology can do and what institutions actually need.

I am drawn to careers in technology consulting and infrastructure investing - helping build and finance the physical and digital backbone that economies run on.

Writing & Analysis

Does Bitcoin Hedge Tech Risk? A Data-Driven Look

Ran 5 years of daily price data to test whether Bitcoin actually hedges technology-sector risk. Found a 0.38 correlation with the NASDAQ and an average -8.8% drawdown during tech selloffs, while gold held flat at -0.1%. Conclusion: BTC amplifies rather than hedges tech exposure.
